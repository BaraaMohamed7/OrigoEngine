Quantum computing leverages quantum mechanical phenomena like superposition and entanglement to process information in fundamentally new ways. Quantum bits called qubits can represent multiple states simultaneously, enabling parallel computation. Quantum algorithms like Shor algorithm factor large numbers exponentially faster than classical methods. Grover search algorithm provides quadratic speedup for unstructured search problems. Quantum error correction techniques protect fragile quantum states from decoherence and noise. Potential applications include drug discovery, materials science, cryptography, and financial modeling. Companies like IBM and Google are building increasingly powerful quantum processors.
