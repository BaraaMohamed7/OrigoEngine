The internet of things connects billions of devices worldwide, creating a vast network of interconnected smart objects. These devices collect and share data, enabling automation and efficiency in homes, industries, and cities. Edge computing processes data closer to the source, reducing latency and bandwidth usage. Cloud computing provides scalable resources on demand, supporting the infrastructure for modern applications.

Smart homes integrate IoT devices for lighting, heating, security, and entertainment control. Industrial IoT optimizes manufacturing through predictive maintenance and real-time monitoring. Smart cities use IoT sensors for traffic management, waste management, and energy efficiency. Wearable devices track health metrics, fitness data, and location information. IoT security challenges include device authentication, data encryption, and network protection. MQTT and CoAP are lightweight protocols for IoT communication. 5G networks enable faster IoT data transmission with lower latency. Digital twins create virtual replicas of physical systems for simulation and analysis. Fog computing extends cloud capabilities to the network edge. IoT platforms provide device management, data processing, and application development tools.
