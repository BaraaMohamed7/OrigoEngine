Data science combines statistics, programming, and domain knowledge to extract insights from data. Data scientists use various tools and techniques to clean, analyze, and visualize data. Big data refers to large, complex datasets that require advanced tools for processing and analysis. Data warehousing stores structured and unstructured data for business intelligence and reporting purposes.

Data mining discovers patterns and relationships in large datasets. Predictive analytics forecasts future trends based on historical data. Descriptive analytics summarizes past data to understand what happened. Diagnostic analytics investigates why something happened. Prescriptive analytics recommends actions to achieve desired outcomes. Data lakes store raw data in its native format for later processing. ETL processes extract, transform, and load data between systems. Data governance ensures data quality, security, and compliance. Python, R, and SQL are essential tools for data scientists. Libraries like pandas, numpy, and scikit-learn facilitate data analysis. Data visualization tools like Tableau and Power BI present insights clearly.
